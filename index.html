The Problem

Three gaps in creator marketing — specific to fintech.

AngelOne runs creator campaigns. The creatives get views. KYC numbers don't always follow. Here's where the system breaks.

01 · Attribution Gap
You can't tell which creator drove a KYC. Or a first trade.
Affiliate links track clicks — that's solved. What isn't solved is connecting organic creator content to business outcomes: which reel drove an app install, which video series lifted KYC completion rates in a cohort, which creator archetype generates accounts that actually trade. The influence exists; the measurement doesn't.
02 · Selection Gap
Creator selection is based on follower count, not audience fit.
A creator with 500K followers in personal finance looks right on paper. But if 60% of their audience is already invested elsewhere or too young to open an account, the campaign will underperform. AngelOne's ideal KYC candidate — 22–35, salaried, first or second brokerage account — isn't evaluated at the creator selection stage at all.
03 · No Learning Loop
Every campaign brief starts from scratch.
There is no real-time feedback from what's working. Which hook drove installs? Which creator archetype completed KYC at the highest rate? Which format converted to a first trade within 7 days? Without a system that captures and feeds this back, the next brief is built on instinct — not on what the last campaign actually taught you. Creator performance never compounds.

Stage 1 — Views
The gap
A creator's 2M followers look right on paper. But if 60% are "passive finance consumers" who'll never open a brokerage account, the views mean nothing. You're paying for reach from the wrong people.
Galvor's lever
Audience-fit scoring on every creator before activation — views from trading-ready segments, not just reach numbers. Select for audience composition, not follower count.
Stage 2 — Page Visits
The gap
High watch-time, low click-through. Hooks that entertain don't always create intent to act. You're getting views but not traffic — and you don't know which hook type drives the click.
Galvor's lever
Hook × click-through analysis from your campaign history — brief creators on hooks that drive link clicks, not just video completions. Creator-specific UTM links capture every visit per creator.
Stage 3 — KYCs
The gap
Users arrive at the app but abandon mid-KYC. You don't know which creators sent the highest-completing users vs which ones sent browsers. Install attribution exists — KYC-completion attribution doesn't.
Galvor's lever
KYC funnel pixel per creator — know exactly which creator drove a completed account, not just an install. Creator-specific landing pages reduce abandonment by pre-filling journey context.
Stage 4 — Trading (LTV)
The gap
KYC ≠ active trader. Funded accounts that never place a trade = zero revenue. You don't know whether Creator A drove accounts that trade daily or accounts that go dormant in week one.
Galvor's lever
7-day and 30-day first-trade events attributed per creator archetype. High-LTV audience profiles feed the next creator selection — the system compounds every campaign.

Questions You'll Ask

Four objections — and the honest answers.

The questions that come up in every fintech pitch. Answered directly, without filler.

Q1
"SEBI just shut down Zerodha's creator programme. Isn't working with influencers risky for us?"
Zero1 was about creators giving investment advice and stock recommendations — that's what triggered SEBI. Galvor optimises brand and educational content performance only — "How AngelOne's platform works" or "Basics of options trading." We don't touch stock tips, we don't predict performance of investment advice. Groww and Upstox are still running creator marketing within these boundaries. The risk is non-compliance — not creator marketing itself.
Q2
"We already track installs via UTM links and promo codes. Why do we need Galvor?"
UTM tracking is measurement after you spend. Galvor predicts before you commit budget. You'll know which creator is likely to drive 5,000 installs vs 500 installs before the campaign goes live — not three months later when the invoice is already paid. The difference is reactive tracking vs predictive optimisation. Both are necessary; only one stops wasted spend.
Q3
"Our creator content is educational, not promotional. Does Galvor even apply here?"
Exactly — educational content is your growth engine. But not all finance educators are equal. Some have audiences of learners (low conversion — they watch, stay curious, don't open accounts). Some have audiences ready to trade (high conversion — they act on what they learn). Galvor predicts which educators convert to installs so you don't waste budget on the wrong archetype. The content being compliant doesn't make every creator equally effective.
Q4
"You've done fashion and D2C. How do we know this works for fintech?"
The Galvor model tracks 1,000+ attributes per creative: audience demographics, engagement patterns, content format, creator authenticity, audience intent signals. These variables apply across categories — the inputs change, the prediction methodology doesn't. MaisonX: ₹50L/month, 18 months, 60% RoAS improvement. Same AI model. We train it on your campaign data, not industry benchmarks. The fintech equivalent is KYC rate, not RoAS — but the system is identical.
